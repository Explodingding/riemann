Environmental protection research hub

Lommel vs Uganda: Sustainable Resource Management

A data-collection page for comparing how Lommel, Belgium manages high-purity silica extraction, ecological reclamation and renewable power with how Uganda could control silica sand resources around Lake Victoria without repeating preventable environmental damage.

Core question

Can Uganda copy the useful parts of the Lommel model?

Yes, but only if the model is adapted: deposit testing, wetland protection, enforceable reclamation, local manufacturing and transparent monitoring must come before rapid extraction.

Lommel lesson

Extraction is managed as an industrial ecosystem

Lommel links processed silica, customer logistics, renewable energy, water recirculation and post-mining nature planning into one long-term system.

Uganda risk

Lake Victoria changes the environmental stakes

Sand mining near wetlands and shorelines can affect erosion, fish habitat, water quality and local livelihoods, especially where informal extraction outpaces enforcement.

Research output

A living dataset, not a one-time essay

The page organizes evidence into metrics, source links, data gaps and policy actions so the project can grow into a fieldwork or dissertation-style study.

Working thesis

Resource control should mean more than stopping illegal mining.

A strong system controls what is extracted, who benefits, how water and biodiversity are protected, where value is added, how much financial assurance is posted, and what the land becomes after mining.
